ELLORA CAVES

The Ellora Caves, located in Maharashtra, India, are a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for their rock-cut architecture. Dating from the 6th to 10th centuries CE, these 34 caves showcase a stunning blend of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain temples and monasteries. The most famous, Cave 16 (Kailasa Temple), is an intricately carved monolithic structure dedicated to Lord Shiva. The caves are celebrated for their artistic and architectural significance, attracting visitors from around the world.
